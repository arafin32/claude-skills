## What it does

Identifies high-intent, low-competition keywords for SEO and content marketing campaigns, with search volume estimates and content recommendations.

---

## How to use it

1. Copy the system prompt below
2. Paste it into Claude
3. Describe your business/topic
4. Get a list of keywords ranked by opportunity
5. Use for content strategy and SEO planning

### Time needed: 3-5 minutes

---

## System Prompt

```
You are an SEO expert and keyword researcher. Your job is to find keywords that drive real traffic and conversions.

When given a business or topic, you will:
1. Generate 30-50 keyword ideas across difficulty levels
2. Categorize by intent (informational, commercial, transactional)
3. Estimate search volume (high/medium/low based on industry knowledge)
4. Assess keyword difficulty (easy/medium/hard to rank for)
5. Identify quick wins (low competition, decent volume)
6. Suggest content format for each keyword

Format your response as:
```

## Content Strategy

### Phase 1: Build Authority (Months 1-2)
Target the Quick Wins section to establish domain authority.

- **"Best Sustainable Water Bottles 2024"** (2000 words)
  - Keywords: sustainable water bottle, eco friendly water bottle, best bamboo bottle
  - Format: Buying guide with product reviews
  - Internal links: To individual product pages

- **"Eco-Friendly Reusable Bottles vs. Disposable Plastic"** (1500 words)
  - Keywords: plastic free water bottle, reusable water bottle, sustainable bottle
  - Format: Comparison guide
  - Hook: Environmental impact statistics

- **"The Best Bamboo Water Bottles"** (1200 words)
  - Keywords: bamboo water bottle, sustainable water bottle, eco friendly
  - Format: Product roundup
  - Include: Customer reviews, sustainability certifications

### Phase 2: Expand Authority (Months 3-4)
Move to Medium Opportunity keywords with internal links from Phase 1 articles.

- **"The Ultimate Water Bottle Buying Guide"** (3000 words)
  - Keywords: best water bottles, reusable water bottles, water bottle brands
  - Format: Comprehensive guide
  - Internal links: Link from Phase 1 articles

- **"Sustainable Water Bottle Brands: Where to Buy"** (2000 words)
  - Keywords: water bottle brands, sustainable brands, eco friendly water bottle
  - Format: Brand guide
  - Include: Comparison table, sustainability ratings

### Phase 3: Monetize & Link (Months 5+)
Leverage domain authority to target higher-competition keywords.

- **"Is Your Water Bottle Actually Eco-Friendly?"** (1800 words)
  - Keywords: eco friendly products, sustainable products, green living
  - Format: Educational deep-dive
  - Hook: Expose "greenwashing" in the industry

## Seasonal Keywords
Search volume spikes at certain times.

- **January:** "New Year sustainability", "reusable water bottle New Year resolution"
- **Spring/Summer:** "Best water bottles for hiking", "hydration bottles for exercise"
- **Back-to-School:** "Water bottles for kids", "durable school water bottle"
- **Holiday:** "Sustainable gifts", "eco-friendly gift ideas", "gift water bottles"

## Related Search Terms
People often search for related terms before/after your main keywords.

**Before (awareness):**
- How much plastic waste is in the ocean?
- Why is single-use plastic bad?
- Best sustainable lifestyle changes

**After (decision):**
- [Brand name] water bottle reviews
- How to clean stainless steel water bottle
- Are bamboo water bottles safe?
- Where to buy eco-friendly water bottles online

## Keyword Difficulty Breakdown

### Easy (Quick Wins - Target First)
- Low search volume (100-300/month)
- Specific long-tail phrases (5+ words)
- New niches or brand-new products
- Local keywords (city-specific)
- Realistic ranking time: 2-4 months

### Medium
- Medium volume (300-1000/month)
- 3-4 word keywords
- Established but not saturated topics
- Requires some domain authority
- Realistic ranking time: 4-8 months

### Hard
- High volume (1000+/month)
- 1-3 word keywords
- Competitive niches with many players
- Requires significant domain authority
- Realistic ranking time: 8-12+ months

## Tips & Variations

### Customize for your niche:
- **Local business:** Add location (e.g., "sustainable water bottles near me")
- **B2B:** Focus on buyer intent (e.g., "bulk eco-friendly bottles for corporate gifts")
- **Specific product:** Long-tail variations ("insulated bamboo bottle that keeps drinks cold 12 hours")
- **Service:** Problem-first keywords ("how to reduce plastic waste", "why drink filtered water")

### Get more insights:
- Ask for "competitor keywords" (what are your competitors ranking for?)
- Request "seasonal content calendar" (what to post when)
- Get "customer search intent analysis" (what problems are they solving?)
- Ask for "keyword clusters" (group related keywords into content pillars)

### Pro tips:
- Start with low-competition keywords for quick wins
- Build internal links from easy keywords to harder ones
- Monitor rankings monthly and adjust strategy
- Look for keywords with commercial intent (people ready to buy)
- Use long-tail keywords for faster ranking
- Create content clusters around keyword families

## Common Issues

**Q: Should I target high-volume keywords first?**
A: No. Target low-competition keywords first to build authority, then expand to higher-volume terms.

**Q: How many keywords per article?**
A: Focus on 1 primary keyword + 3-5 related keywords. Don't keyword stuff.

**Q: These volume estimates — where do they come from?**
A: Based on industry knowledge and broader trends. Use Google Search Console and Ahrefs for exact data if you need precision.

**Q: Should I go after competitor keywords?**
A: Yes, especially variations they might have missed. But don't copy their strategy entirely.

**Q: How do I know if a keyword will convert?**
A: Look at search intent (is it a buying keyword?). Test small articles on low-volume keywords first.

## Notes

- These are estimates based on industry knowledge. Use SEO tools (Ahrefs, SEMrush, Moz) for exact data.
