## What it does

Generates comprehensive, SEO-optimized blog post outlines with section headers, key points, and suggested content for each section.

---

## How to use it

1. Copy the system prompt below
2. Paste it into Claude
3. Provide your blog topic and keywords
4. Get a detailed outline ready to write from
5. Use as a roadmap for writing

### Time needed: 3-5 minutes

---

## System Prompt

```
You are an expert content strategist and SEO specialist. Your job is to create detailed blog post outlines that rank well and keep readers engaged.

When given a blog topic and keywords, you will:
1. Create an SEO-optimized outline with clear hierarchy
2. Include an engaging introduction hook
3. Break content into logical sections with subheadings
4. Add 3-5 key points per section
5. Include a strong call-to-action
6. Optimize for target keywords naturally

Format your response as:

## [Blog Post Title] (SEO-optimized title with keyword)

**Target Keywords:** [Keywords you're optimizing for]
**Estimated Read Time:** [Minutes]
**Target Audience:** [Who this is for]

---

## Outline

### Introduction (Hook)
[Opening line that grabs attention]

**Points to cover:**
- [Point 1]
- [Point 2]

---

### Section 1: [Heading with keyword if possible]
[Brief description of what this section covers]

**Key points:**
- [Point 1 with supporting detail]
- [Point 2]
- [Point 3]

**Content ideas:**
- [Specific example or statistic]
- [Case study or real-world application]

---

[Continue for 4-6 main sections]

---

### Conclusion & CTA
[Summary of key takeaways]

**Call to Action:**
- [What you want reader to do next]

---

**SEO Checklist:**
- [ ] Title includes primary keyword
- [ ] H2 headers include related keywords naturally
- [ ] Internal link opportunities identified
- [ ] 2000+ word outline (adjust as needed)
- [ ] Content answers search intent

Remember: This is a detailed outline to work from. Write naturally and add personality as you flesh it out.
```

## Example Input

```
Topic: "How to Start a Sustainable Business"
Keywords: sustainable business, eco-friendly startup, green business ideas, sustainable practices
Target Audience: Entrepreneurs and small business owners interested in sustainability
Target Length: 2000-2500 words
```

## Example Output

## How to Start a Sustainable Business: The Complete Guide for Eco-Conscious Entrepreneurs

**Target Keywords:** sustainable business, eco-friendly startup, green business, sustainable practices, environmentally friendly
**Estimated Read Time:** 8-10 minutes
**Target Audience:** First-time entrepreneurs and small business owners who want to build profitable, environmentally responsible companies

---

## Outline

### Introduction: Why Sustainable Business Matters Now
Your opening hook could be a statistic about consumer demand for eco-friendly products or a personal story about why sustainability matters.

**Key points to cover:**
- 73% of millennials and Gen Z prefer sustainable brands (sets urgency)
- Sustainable businesses often see 20%+ higher profit margins (sets motivation)
- Sustainability isn't a trend, it's the future (legitimizes the approach)
- You don't need to sacrifice profitability to be green (removes objection)

**Content ideas:**
- Open with a powerful stat about consumer preference for sustainable products
- Share a real example of a successful sustainable startup (Patagonia, Who Gives A Crap, etc.)
- End with: "Here's how to build your own sustainable business..."

---

### Section 1: Define Your Sustainable Business Idea

**Description:** Help readers identify what sustainable business model fits their values and the market.

**Key points:**
- Sustainability covers 3 areas: environmental, social, economic (not just "green")
- Choose a problem you actually care about solving (passion = durability)
- Research the market size and competitor landscape
- Identify your unique angle or approach
- Consider your personal skills and resources

**Content ideas:**
- Give 5-7 examples of sustainable business ideas (eco-products, services, consulting, impact-focused)
- Explain "triple bottom line" thinking (people, planet, profit)
- Include a "values audit" exercise readers can do
- Show 2-3 competitor examples (what they're doing right, opportunities you see)

---

### Section 2: Research Your Market & Validate Demand

**Description:** Help readers avoid building something nobody wants.

**Key points:**
- Sustainable products have higher willingness-to-pay (customers pay premium for values)
- Start with primary research (survey, interviews, social listening)
- Identify your specific target audience (not "everyone")
- Understand pricing expectations and margins
- Check competitor pricing and positioning
- Look for underserved niches

**Content ideas:**
- Step-by-step guide to customer interviews (5-10 good interviews beat 100 surveys)
- Tools for market research (Google Trends, social media, competitor analysis)
- Case study: How one founder validated demand before launching
- Template for a simple one-page market assessment

---

### Section 3: Build Your Sustainable Business Model

**Description:** Show readers how to structure profitability + impact.

**Key points:**
- Sustainable doesn't mean non-profit (you need profits to survive and scale)
- Map your value chain and find sustainable sourcing options
- Plan pricing that covers costs + your margin + premium for impact
- Consider supply chain transparency and certifications
- Build in metrics to track environmental/social impact
- Plan for scaling sustainably (don't compromise for growth)

**Content ideas:**
- Sustainable sourcing strategy (fair trade, local, circular, recycled)
- Pricing model examples from successful eco-brands
- Impact metrics frameworks (carbon footprint, water saved, waste diverted, lives impacted)
- Common mistakes (under-pricing, greenwashing, unsustainable suppliers)

---

### Section 4: Handle Operations & Supply Chain Responsibly

**Description:** The unsexy but critical part of building sustainably.

**Key points:**
- Your supply chain determines 70%+ of your environmental impact
- Build relationships with suppliers who share your values
- Get certifications if relevant (B Corp, Fair Trade, ISO, etc.)
- Plan packaging and shipping to minimize waste
- Build in feedback loops to continuously improve
- Be transparent about where you fall short
- Set science-based targets (not just vague "green" goals)

**Content ideas:**
- Interview with a sustainable supply chain manager
- Common sustainable certifications and which ones matter for your industry
- Checklist for vetting suppliers
- Packaging options comparison (cost, impact, customer perception)

---

### Section 5: Marketing Your Sustainable Business Without Greenwashing

**Description:** Tell your story authentically without overstating your impact.

**Key points:**
- Consumers are skeptical of "green" marketing (they've seen too much greenwashing)
- Authenticity and transparency beat marketing gimmicks
- Show your impact with real numbers and evidence
- Be honest about what you DON'T do yet
- Tell the "why" behind your choices
- Build community around shared values
- Use content and education as your primary marketing

**Content ideas:**
- Definition and examples of greenwashing (what to avoid)
- "Sustainability transparency" checklist (what to share with customers)
- Social proof examples (customer testimonials focused on impact)
- Content marketing strategy for eco-conscious audiences
- Partnership opportunities (other sustainable brands, nonprofits, influencers)

---

### Section 6: Launch, Measure, & Continuously Improve

**Description:** How to actually get started and track progress.

**Key points:**
- Launch small and validate before scaling (MVP approach)
- Set impact metrics from day one (not after you're big)
- Measure both environmental and business metrics
- Get customer feedback on values alignment
- Be prepared to pivot based on learning
- Plan for sustainable growth (don't burn out)
- Share results and learnings publicly

**Content ideas:**
- Simple impact tracking template (Excel or free tools)
- Launch timeline (6 months to launch is typical for thoughtful startups)
- Growth strategy that doesn't sacrifice values
- How to communicate impact to customers monthly/quarterly
- Common growth pitfalls for sustainable businesses

---

### Conclusion: Your Sustainable Business Journey Starts Now

**Summary of key takeaways:**
- Sustainable business is viable and growing (not niche anymore)
- Start with a problem you care about and validate demand
- Build profitability and impact together (not tradeoffs)
- Your supply chain is where the real impact happens
- Authenticity and transparency matter more than marketing

**Call to Action:**
- "Ready to launch? Here's a 90-day sustainable startup checklist..." (link to lead magnet)
- "Share your sustainable business idea in the comments below"
- "Subscribe to learn about more eco-conscious entrepreneurship"
- "Join our community of sustainable founders" (community link)

---

## Writing Notes

**Tone:** Encouraging but realistic. You're teaching people how to build a real business, not romanticizing sustainability.

**Structure tips:**
- Use subheadings frequently for skimmability
- Include data and statistics to build credibility
- Use examples from real companies (both wins and failures)
- Break up text with lists, tables, images
- End sections with transitions to next section

**Length:** This outline is about 2000-2500 words when fleshed out. Adjust sections up/down based on your target length.

---

## Tips & Variations

### Customize for different topics:
- **How-to guides:** More steps, checklists, templates
- **Opinion/thought leadership:** More data, counterarguments, original research
- **Product/service guides:** More comparisons, pros/cons, recommendations
- **Tutorial/technical:** More screenshots, code examples, step-by-step

### Make it stronger:
- Ask for "internal linking strategy" to connect related posts
- Request "competitor analysis" to find gaps you can cover
- Get "LSI keywords" (semantically related keywords) for better SEO
- Ask for "long-form data points" (statistics that support each section)

### Pro tips:
- Write the outline before you start writing (saves time)
- Use H2 for major sections, H3 for subsections (SEO best practice)
- Aim for 2000-3000 words for competitive keywords
- Include real examples, data, and case studies (beats generic advice)
- Optimize title after writing (you might discover better angles)

## Common Issues

**Q: The outline is too long/short**
A: Adjust the number of main sections or depth per section

**Q: Keywords don't fit naturally**
A: Don't force them. Natural writing ranks better than keyword stuffing.

**Q: I don't know what to write in each section**
A: The outline gives you the structure. Research + personal experience fill in the details.

**Q: How do I know if it's SEO-optimized?**
A: Check that title and H2s include target keywords, content answers search intent, and it's at least 2000 words for competitive terms.
