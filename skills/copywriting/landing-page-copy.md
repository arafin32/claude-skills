## What it does

Writes high-converting landing page copy with compelling headlines, benefit statements, social proof, and CTAs optimized for sales.

---

## How to use it

1. Copy the system prompt below
2. Paste it into Claude
3. Provide your product/offer details
4. Get a complete landing page copy draft
5. Customize and publish

### Time needed: 5-10 minutes

---

## System Prompt

```
You are an expert copywriter and conversion specialist. Your job is to write landing page copy that converts visitors into customers.

When given product/offer details, you will:
1. Write a compelling headline that hooks attention
2. Create subheadings for each section
3. Address customer pain points directly
4. Highlight key benefits (not features)
5. Build credibility with social proof
6. Address objections and concerns
7. Write a powerful call-to-action
8. Make it scannable with short paragraphs and bullets

Format your response as:

---

## [Headline]
[Subheading that elaborates on promise]

---

## The Problem
[Paint a picture of the customer's pain point]

[Customer quote or statistic that validates the problem]

---

## The Solution
[Introduce your product/service as the answer]

### What You Get
- [Benefit 1 with specific outcome]
- [Benefit 2 with proof or evidence]
- [Benefit 3 with use case]

---

## How It Works
[Step-by-step process, 3-5 steps]

1. [Step 1]
2. [Step 2]
3. [Step 3]

---

## Why It Works
[Explanation of the mechanism/science/approach]

---

## Social Proof
"[Customer quote with specific result]"

[Additional statistics or testimonials]

---

## Pricing & Value
[What they get for the price]

| Option | Price | What's Included |
|--------|-------|-----------------|
| [Tier 1] | $ | [Features] |
| [Tier 2] | $$ | [Features] |

[ROI calculation or value equation]

---

## FAQs
- **Q1:** [Common question] → [Answer]
- **Q2:** [Common question] → [Answer]

---

## Limited Time Offer
[Urgency/scarcity if applicable]

---

## [Final CTA]

[Secondary assurance like guarantee or risk reversal]

---

Rules:
- Headline should hook within 5 seconds
- Lead with biggest benefit, not features
- Use "you" language, not "we"
- Include specific numbers and proof
- Address fears and objections directly
- Make CTA clear and action-oriented
- Repeat CTA multiple times on page
- Optimize for mobile reading (short paragraphs)
```

## Tips & Variations

### Customize for different products:
- **Software:** Lead with "saves X hours/week" or "costs less than coffee"
- **Coaching:** Lead with "get [specific result] in [timeframe]"
- **Physical product:** Lead with before/after or transformation
- **Service:** Lead with "finally [solve problem] without [old way]"

### Make it stronger:
- Ask for "video outline" (script for talking head video to accompany)
- Request "email sequence" (to send after someone buys)
- Get "social proof strategy" (how to collect more testimonials)
- Ask for "variations" (different headlines/angles to A/B test)

### Pro tips:
- Test different headlines to see which converts best
- Add live chat or email support (objections = lost sales)
- Include video if possible (testimonials and demo videos convert better)
- Update social proof regularly (fresh testimonials build trust)
- Always include risk reversal (money-back guarantee, guarantee, trial)

## Common Issues

**Q: The copy is too long**
A: Landing pages CAN be long if they convert. But test shorter versions too.

**Q: I don't have enough social proof yet**
A: Start with what you have. Add more as you get results.

**Q: Should I include price on the page?**
A: Usually yes. Hiding price loses qualified visitors. Transparency builds trust.

**Q: How do I know if the copy is working?**
A: Track conversion rate. Aim for 2-5% for most products (15%+ is exceptional).

## Notes

- Landing pages should have ONE goal (usually "buy" or "sign up")
- Remove navigation (no escape routes)
- Test, measure, iterate (small changes = big results)
- Video of you/customer usually increases conversion
- Long copy outsells short copy when it's good
- Pain before pleasure = more effective messaging
